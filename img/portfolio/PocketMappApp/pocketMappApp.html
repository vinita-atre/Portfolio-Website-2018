Engagaing Millenails in Parks- Contextual Design

Scenario

The Montogomery Parks want to get millenials to go to the park and increase awareness about parks. They also want to increase use of park programs and facilities as well as technological engagement in the parks. I and my team used contextual design methods to conduct user resaerch, analyze the data, gather insights and suggest product concepts to the clients. Here is an article that talks about our product concept's presentation to the clients.

1.Background Research

Through our literature review, we found that..

  1. Majority of Millenials value parks highly for the nature and ability to relax
  2. Millenials have differing opinions on leisure time ranging from tranquility to excitment and newness
  3. Ease of travel to parks is important.
  4. Going to parks as kids leads to going as adults.


2.Field Research

Meet our Users:

We conducted 2 hour deep contextual interviews with millenails. We recruited particiapnts through social media and our own network. We interviewed 12 users in total with 4 men and 8 women. They were in teh age range of 21 years to 35 years. Our participants had a variety of occupations - student, nanny, artist, Government agent,TA, unemployed, Pizza delivery. After each interview, within 48 hours an interpretation session for that interview was held with the team. The interviewer narated while interview notes were written, user's Identity Model and Park's Physical Model were drawn.

Problem Overview

We found several issues that come in the way of millenials engaging with parks. They can be seen across various aspects of a millenial's life.

Creating Affinity Diagrams

After each interview, we conducted interpretation sessions to capture notes and experience models for each of our users. We collected over 700 notes to construct our affinity model from a bootom to top hierarchy.

The complete Affinity model was made online using Lucid Charts and can be found here. From the top level green post its in our affinity, we found our key challenges.

Challenges and high level findings

  1. Millenials research the parks they go to
  2. Users mostly go with their friends
  3. Many users don’t know about the parks near them
  4. Millennials want to be able to “Just Go” to the park on a whim
  5. Users want to be immersed in park
  6. Users don’t want to do "just" tech things in park
  7. Users create or play games in the park
  8. Millenials go to the parks for intense physical activity
  9. How users grew up with Parks determines how they use them
  10. Users don’t want to have to leave parks for their basic needs
  11. Users want a social parks' experience
  12. It’s really about what people are in the park
  13. Millenials want safety in the parks

Creating Experience Models

We created a two models - Identity model and Park's Phyical model.

A.The identity model shows the various identities or "things that are core to me and I take pride in". A user may have one or more identity elements. The consolidated Identity model is created from the individual Identity models for each user created during the interpretation session.


B.The Physical model shows what activities and issues arise in different focus areas on the maps. The consolidated Park's Physical model is from individual physical models for each user interview.

3.Ideation

After organizing our data, we created a list of "hot issues". We then created visions where we redefine the day-in-life for our user to solve the key issues. They were sketched out in the form of stories. These visions highlighted opportunities for product concepts. From the 4 visions, we then created 4 product concepts.

1. pocket Map App: Addressing issues like social experience and also tranquility, information about parks and facilities in parks, creating-viewing-sharing content in parks, tagging parks with cool activities to improve park's brand and awareness. The app also provides an SOS feature to call park staff, handsfree immersion mode to guide user through the interesting things in the park through location detection and voice.

2.Parks Rental System: Millenials want low prep visits to parks and view parks as places to play, hike and carry out various activities alone or with friends. The rental system provides a centralized equipment loan service with an app and kiosk interface. There will be smart lockers at parks for easy renting and returning of play equipment.

3.Parks Arts System: To provide excuse to the park user and non-user alike as well as address needs of community space enthusiast, we created arts system. The app will deliver a social experience of visiting music, theatre and art events to users. This will also increase awareness of the parks. Also by allowing users to request event creaton, they can be better regulated by parks staff and lead to creation of self-activated spaces.
